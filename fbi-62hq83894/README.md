# FBI 62-HQ-83894: Master Report

**File name:** Flying Discs / Flying Saucers
**Span:** 1944 (retrospective) through 1973
**Pages:** 1,262 OCR'd, six sections (2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 10)
**Released:** May 8, 2026 as a component of [PURSUE Release 01](../pursue-release-01/)
**Method:** 50+ standalone cases mined across 41 archival passes (May 8–10, 2026)
**Inventory:** [FBI 62-HQ-83894 case index](full-inventory.md)

## TL;DR

The FBI's flying-discs case file is the single most important historical UFO archive ever declassified, and it has been hiding in plain sight on the FBI Vault since 2011 in the form of one over-cited memo (the Hottel "three saucers" memo) that does not say what the internet thinks it says.

PURSUE Release 01 included the full file, OCR-able and grep-able for the first time. What is in there is not "proof of aliens." What is in there is the actual paper trail of how the US Government first encountered, classified, investigated, and then quietly abandoned the flying-saucer problem between 1947 and 1953, and then half-heartedly logged civilian correspondence about it for the next twenty years.

The four threads that organize the file:

1. **[1947, the origin year.](#thread-1-1947-the-origin-year)** Kenneth Arnold's June 24 Cascade sighting is in the file with Frank M. Brown's CIC credibility assessment intact. The FBI's first institutional response patterns are formed in summer 1947. By August 19, the FBI Liaison Section and Air Force Intelligence are already privately discussing whether the saucers are a classified US Government experiment.

2. **[1948–1949, institutional hardening.](#thread-2-19481949-institutional-hardening)** The Mantell P-51 fatality. Air Defense Command's institutional baseline. Project Grudge and the Vital Installations correspondence. Oak Ridge atomic-installation overflights. The Cuneo / Jones / Winchell political-relay channel that shows what *did* trigger Bureau action: source proximity, not source content.

3. **[1949–1950, the disinformation year.](#thread-3-19491950-the-disinformation-year)** Project Twinkle gets approved at Holloman / White Sands. The Belmont master memo logs every OSI sighting from 1948 through 1950. Stuart Adcock files an Oak Ridge incoming-message cluster. SAC Pervier corroborates a Tulsa case as a former FBI agent on duty. The Hottel memo arrives in the same week as all of that and gets filed without analytical action. Hoover then orders a Communist-affiliation cross-check on the author of *Behind the Flying Saucers*, Frank Scully, not because of the saucers, but because Scully is already in the LA Office's late-1930s subject file.

4. **[1950s–1973, the long tail.](#thread-4-1950s1973-the-long-tail)** The Hoover-directed civilian-correspondence pattern: politely decline to investigate. Section 10 catches the 1966–1973 post-Blue Book civilian writers, including the James Collins Chesapeake abduction claim from January 1967.

Read carefully, the file does not show the FBI hiding aliens. It shows the FBI doing intake on a problem they decided early was not theirs to solve, kicking it to the Air Force, and then logging the rumor traffic that kept showing up.

### Pre-Roswell

### The Arnold event and its immediate aftermath

- **[Kenneth Arnold, Cascade Mountains, June 24, 1947](cases/kenneth-arnold-cascade-1947.md)**: The origin event of the modern UFO phenomenon. Nine objects in chain formation, 1 minute 42 second high-plateau traversal, 9,500 feet altitude. CIC investigator Frank M. Brown's credibility assessment is in the file: *"All statements made by Mr. Arnold in regard to the distances involved, speed of the objects, course of the objects and size of the objects, could very possibly be facts."* FBI serial 62-83894-95.
- **[Kenneth Arnold sighting narrative](cases/kenneth-arnold-sighting-narrative-cascade-june-1947.md)** and **[biographical statement](cases/kenneth-arnold-biographical-statement-1947.md)**: The narrative and biographical components of the same investigation.
- **[Hatfield-Ellison, Myrtle Creek Oregon, August 6, 1947](cases/hatfield-ellison-myrtle-creek-1947.md)**: A US Naval Air Corps Lt JG with 3.5 years of Atlantic submarine patrol, plus a student pilot, two separate aircraft takeoffs, instrumental speed computation 1,000 mph. Highest civilian-pilot computed speed in the 1947 wave. Near-zero institutional follow-up despite credentials.
- **[Maury Island 1947](cases/maury-island-1947.md)**: The famous Maury Island hoax in primary form.
- **[Springer / Brown CIC cluster, pre-Maury Island, 1947](cases/springer-brown-cic-cluster-pre-maury-island-1947.md)**: The FBI / CIC investigative cluster that preceded the Maury Island case.
- **[Davidson / Brown crash mission report](cases/davidson-brown-crash-mission-report.md)**: The fatal CIC crash that ended the Maury Island investigation.
- **[Rhodes Phoenix photographs, 1947](cases/rhodes-phoenix-photographs-1947.md)**: The William Rhodes Phoenix photographs case.
- **[Muroc 1947 CIC affidavits](cases/muroc-1947-cic-affidavits.md)**: Affidavits from the Muroc Army Air Field (later Edwards AFB) cluster.
- **[Raymond Lane, luminous paint and radioactive sand, July 1947](cases/raymond-lane-luminous-paint-radioactive-sand-july-1947.md)**: Dow Chemical employee, Midland Michigan field, a "ball of white" burning a foot off the ground. Dow Physics Laboratory analyzed the fused sand. Witness has 1924 burglary conviction. Material handed to Army Air Forces Intelligence at Selfridge Field.
- **[Sarbanis radio-ham contactee alien message, July 1947](cases/sarbanis-radio-ham-contactee-alien-message-july-1947.md)**: The earliest documented FBI institutional treatment of a UFO contactee narrative. Coded message in *Newsday* signed "Radio Ham," decoded by Sarbanis, verified by FBI Lab as "substantially correct." Assessed as a hoax. Important for what it shows about how the Bureau treated the contactee genre from week one.
- **[Government secret experiment theory, August 1947](cases/government-secret-experiment-theory-august-1947.md)**: The Fitch-Reynolds-Garrett inter-agency discussion of August 19, 1947. FBI Liaison Section and Air Force Intelligence privately assess the classified-US-experiment hypothesis as "probability." Garrett's argument: Swedish sightings triggered War Department pressure on intelligence; US domestic sightings did not, suggesting classified knowledge. *"Extremely embarrassing"* exposure if disclosed. Earliest documented inter-agency hypothesis vetting at highest decision-making levels.

### The Hottel memo, properly read

→ **[Guy Hottel "three saucers" memo, March 22, 1950](cases/guy-hottel-three-saucers-new-mexico-1950.md)**

The single most-downloaded document on the FBI Vault. Routinely paraphrased online as *"FBI memo confirms three flying saucers and alien bodies recovered in New Mexico."*

The actual document, read in primary form with the routing chain visible:

- An unnamed *"investigator for the Air Forces"* told Karl Howe, Special Investigator on the **Sex Squad** of the DC Metropolitan Police Department.
- Howe told SA R. H. Kurtzman, FBI Washington Field Office.
- Kurtzman reported it to SAC Hottel.
- Hottel forwarded it to Hoover.
- Bureau disposition, on the same page, in one sentence: ***"No further evaluation was attempted by SA KURTZMAN concerning the above."***

The popular reading inverts the document. It is one of the lowest-confidence single artifacts in 62-HQ-83894: a four-hop rumor with explicit "no further evaluation," in a section dominated by higher-confidence material. The popular treatment of this single page as the headline FBI flying-saucer document is itself a finding about how the Vault is read.

The "metallic cloth" / "blackout suits" detail also dates the rumor to 1950 human-aviation imagination, the vocabulary of G-suits and high-altitude pressure suits in development at Wright-Patterson, transposed onto small occupants. Whoever invented this rumor was a 1950 American reaching for 1950 aviation language, not a transmission from elsewhere.

→ See the [full case writeup](cases/guy-hottel-three-saucers-new-mexico-1950.md) for the routing chain, the four substantive paragraphs, and the eight Why-It-Matters findings.

## What this file is not

- **It is not "the FBI investigated UFOs."** The Bureau's posture from 1947 forward was that this is the Air Force's problem. The FBI did intake. The FBI did not investigate.
- **It is not a hidden alien-recovery file.** The most evidentiarily anchored material in the section is mundane administrative traffic: institutional baseline policies, civilian correspondence patterns, multi-agency liaison protocols, and the OSI cumulative log.
- **It is not "the truth."** It is the paper trail of one agency's role in a multi-agency response to a problem that ran from 1947 through about 1953 with declining institutional interest. The Air Force's Blue Book file is bigger and more substantively relevant to the actual phenomenon.

---

## What this file *is*

- **The most complete institutional ethnography of how a US federal agency handles a sudden unknown.** Read end-to-end, the file is a master class in 1947–1953 inter-agency anxiety, jurisdictional negotiation, and bureaucratic disposition.
- **The earliest documentation of the contactee genre as institutional intake.** The Sarbanis radio-ham case (July 1947) is the FBI Lab verifying a "decoded alien message", and assessing it as a hoax, within weeks of the Roswell press cycle.
- **Empirical evidence on what triggered Bureau action.** The Cuneo / Jones / Winchell channel and the Hottel memo are the operational counterexamples. Source proximity to political power moved files. Source quality, on its own, did not.
- **A correctly-read Hottel memo.** The most-downloaded document on the FBI Vault, in its actual context, is one of the lowest-confidence single artifacts in 62-HQ-83894. That correction matters.

## Method note

The 58 case writeups in this section were extracted from the 1,262 OCR'd pages across 41 mining passes between May 8 and May 10, 2026, using a CIC investigative-priority + high-signal grep + cluster-adjacency methodology. The methodology favors:

- Credentialed witnesses (military aviators, FBI agents, federal investigators)
- Multi-agency coordination patterns
- Geographically clustered cases (atomic installations, vital infrastructure)
- Institutional-disposition documents (policy memos, baseline correspondence patterns)

Sections 3, 4, 5, 6, and 9 are still being mined. New cases will be added as they are extracted.

For every case here, the source citation is in the page's YAML frontmatter (`source:` field) and resolves to a specific page-####.md in [`../raw/fbi-62hq83894-ocr/`](../raw/fbi-62hq83894-ocr/). Quote freely. Cite back to the page.
