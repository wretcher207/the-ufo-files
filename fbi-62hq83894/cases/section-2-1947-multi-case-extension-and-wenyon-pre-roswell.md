## Summary

Section 2 of FBI 62-HQ-83894 contains six standalone 1947 cases (and one September-1946 pre-Roswell case) that sit outside the Springer-Brown 4AF CIC Hamilton Field cluster (`2026-05-08-fbi-62hq83894-springer-brown-cic-cluster-pre-maury-island-1947`) and are individually substantive enough to merit primary-source preservation. Three of them close open threads from pass 23.

This source page captures them as one ingest because they share a common thread: they document the **Bureau's contemporaneous case-handling posture across the August-September 1947 period** — the moment when Bureau Bulletin #42 of July 30, 1947 had just established field-office authority to conduct UAP witness interviews in coordination with the AAF, and the FBI was building its first generation of primary-source case files in real-time.

The six cases are:

1. **Twin Falls Idaho — H. H. Hedstrom and Three Police Officers, August 19, 1947** (Section 2 pages 177-179, FBI serial 62-83894-96). Multi-witness sighting of multiple groups of objects in triangular formation over Twin Falls, including a 35-50 object formation. Three Twin Falls Police Department officers (Detective Richard A. Frazier, M. E. Rountree, Richard Scott) joined the original three civilian witnesses (Hedstrom, Mrs. Hedstrom, Mrs. Henry Shultz). SAC Butte memo of September 2, 1947, with the rare investigator's-discipline finding that the witnesses had ruled out "meteors or comets," "geese or ducks," "reflection of the city lights on some object or objects," and that "**none of the persons who claimed to have seen these objects were drinking at the time.**"

2. **Bethel Alaska — Captain Jack Peck and co-pilot Vince Daly, August 4, 1947** (Section 2 pages 180-181, FBI serial 62-83894-97). SAC San Francisco memo of September 4, 1947 transmitting an attachment from Lt. Col. Donald L. Springer, A-2 Hamilton Field, dated August 28, 1947. Anchorage Field Office formally tasked with separately interviewing Captain Jack Peck and co-pilot Vince Daly per **Bureau Bulletin #42 of July 30, 1947** — the post-Roswell shift in the Bureau's posture. **This page closes the pass-23 "Bethel Alaska NCOIC letter" open thread** by establishing that the Bethel Alaska content lives at Section 2 page 180, not page 182.

3. **Lake Mead Nevada — 1st Lt. Eric B. Armstrong, P-51 pilot, June 28, 1947** (Section 2 page 189, FBI serial 62-2938-11). MOIC at Williams Field, Chandler Arizona, dated 15 July 1947, signed by **Lawrence R. King, Jr., Special Agent, CIC, ADC**, certified true copy by Major R. D. Stephens. **This page closes both the pass-23 Armstrong open thread AND the pass-23 King CIC ADC memo open thread simultaneously.** Five or six white circular objects, ~36 inches in diameter, ~30 miles northwest of Lake Mead Nevada, at 6,000 ft below Armstrong's 10,000 ft, estimated speed 285 MPH. Armstrong was sure the objects were not birds and not jets or conventional aircraft.

4. **Myrtle Creek Oregon — Ray Virgil Hatfield (Lt. JG U.S. Naval Air Corps, 3.5 yrs Atlantic submarine patrol) and Noble Ellison, August 6, 1947** (Section 2 page 52, FBI serial 62-83894-65). SAC Portland memo of August 23, 1947 with subject line "**FLYING DISCS / SECURITY MATTER - X**." Hatfield, operator of Tri City Airport, was instructing student Ellison on takeoff. Spherical aluminum-sheeting object 5,000-8,000 ft, ~30-50 ft diameter, ~1,000 MPH estimated on aircraft computer. Hatfield took over controls to investigate. **Both Hatfield AND Ellison saw the object on the second takeoff at the same time** — direct corroboration on a single flight. Disappearance pattern: rapid East-then-up climb until vanishing in ~45 seconds (Ellison's account).

5. **McLeod / Seattle Times Letter and General Hale's August 25, 1947 AAF Policy Statement** (Section 2 page 190, FBI serial 62-83894-99 / 62-2938). SAC SF memo to Director August 27, 1947. Henry McLeod, city editor of the Seattle Times, wrote to 4AF Hamilton Field asking whether any governmental agency would issue an official statement on flying discs and whether his reporters could interview Arnold, Dahl, and Smith. Springer telephonically advised SA Michael J. Cassidy of the SF FBI Office that **General Hale answered McLeod personally on August 25, 1947** with: AAF "had no information which would definitely clear up the situation," "Mr. McLEOD could feel free to interview whomsoever he desired, in that **there was no censorship at the present time**," "in the interest of economy, the Fourth Air Force Headquarters would not run out every lead or report of flying discs, but that if well-founded and apparently reliable information were received, that such information would be given attention." This is the **earliest in-archive primary-source AAF policy statement on flying-disc reporting and press access**, in writing, three weeks after Davidson and Brown died.

6. **Forrest Wenyon Pre-Roswell Pilot Sightings, September 1946 + October 1946 + June 2, 1947** (Section 2 pages 192-194, FBI serial 62-83894 [stub]). SAC Baltimore memo of September 4, 1947 transmitting a Morning News (Delaware) clipping from July 8, 1947 with the headline "Flying Mayonnaise Jars Reported by Rehoboth Pilot — Forrest Wenyon Believes Mystery Discs, Jet-Propelled, May Have Caused Big Plane Crash on Memorial Day." Wenyon, 30 years' flying experience, sighted three pre-Arnold-period objects, the first one **ten months before Arnold's June 24 1947 sighting**. The Bureau record preserves that **Wenyon called the FBI; "they were not interested and told him so"** — the pre-Bureau-Bulletin-#42 baseline FBI disposition. Plus the loaded theory: Wenyon believed one of these objects caused the **Eastern Airlines Memorial Day 1947 plane crash at Port Deposit, MD that killed 53 persons.**

These six cases were filed in 62-HQ-83894 across SAC offices in Butte, San Francisco, Williams Field/CIC ADC, Portland, and Baltimore — establishing that the Bureau's case-collection footprint for the post-Arnold UAP corpus was nationwide by early September 1947.

## What Each Case Documents

### Case 1 — Twin Falls Hedstrom (Section 2 pages 177, 178, 179)

SAC Butte memo of September 2, 1947 to Director FBI. Subject "FLYING DISCS." H. H. Hedstrom, Executive Director of the Twin Falls Housing Authority, residing at 322 Third Avenue North, Twin Falls, Idaho, was sitting on his front porch with his wife and Mrs. Henry Shultz at approximately 9:30 P.M., August 19, 1947. The sighting sequence:

> "Mrs. HEDSTROM suddenly shouted and pointed to the sky and thereafter stated that she had seen an object traveling at a terrific rate of speed in a northeasterly direction. The object was out of sight before Mr. HEDSTROM and Mrs. SHULTZ could see anything."

Then ten minutes later:

> "all three saw ten similar objects proceeding rapidly in the same direction in the form of a triangle. As the group of objects was disappearing, three of the objects on the left flank peeled off and proceeded in a more northerly direction. The remaining objects appeared to close ranks and proceeded in a northeasterly direction."

Then a second formation, then a third (5-6 objects), then **a 35-50 object formation**:

> "A few minutes later the three persons saw a large group of objects estimated at from thirty-five to fifty flying in a triangular formation in the same direction. Approximately twenty or twenty-five minutes after the large group of objects was observed, similar objects were noted coming back over the city in a southwesterly direction. These objects were generally in groups of three, five and seven and followed each other at approximately five minute intervals."

The witness expansion to law enforcement:

> "Mrs. HEDSTROM called Detective RICHARD A. FRAZIER of the Twin Falls Police Department, a next door neighbor, who was then accompanied by M. E. ROUNTREE and RICHARD SCOTT, also members of the Twin Falls Police Department. The three officers watched for a few minutes and observed a group of about twelve objects flying in formation over the city in a southwesterly direction."

The Butte SAC's investigator-discipline finding (page 179):

> "On the night in question the sky was overcast and the objects could not be seen closely; however, they were distinct enough so that all persons could observe what they described as a glow going through the air. They said that the objects appeared to be lighted from the inside and were of a color similar to regular electric lights."
>
> "Detective FRAZIER and Mr. HEDSTROM both stated that they could not be meteors or comets, that they flew on a level line and did not appear to drop. They flew at a terrific rate of speed and were visible for only a few minutes at a time. Both persons interviewed said that the objects could not have been geese or ducks and that the lights were not a reflection of the city lights on some object or objects."
>
> "None of the persons noticed any flames or anything except the glow of lights. **None of the persons who claimed to have seen these objects were drinking at the time.**"

Disposition: "No further investigation is being conducted by the Butte Office, and the case is being closed." Stamped 62-83894-96. November 18 1964 destruction stamp ("COPIES DESTROYED 270 NOV 18 1964") — this is a **second confirmed instance of the November 1964 destruction-stamp** beyond the Frank Scully URGENT teletype (pass 21) and the Kodiak ONI report (pass 22), strengthening the Bureau-wide retention-housekeeping interpretation.

Page 178 OCR-rendered the body text as illegible "[illegible]" placeholders with one badly OCR'd witness-name fragment ("MONTAGNE" — likely the OCR garbling "HEDSTROM" or "MR. HEDSTROM"). The body of the secondary memo is not OCR-recovered.

### Case 2 — Bethel Alaska Peck/Daly (Section 2 pages 180, 181)

SAC San Francisco memo to Director FBI, dated September 4, 1947, marked AIRMAIL SPECIAL DELIVERY:

> "Enclosed is a copy of a letter dated August 28, 1947, from Lt. Col. DONALD L. SPRINGER of A-2, Hamilton Field, Calif., with an attachment referring to the sighting of a flying object by Captain JACK PECK and his co-pilot, VINCE DALY, near Bethel, Alaska."
>
> "Photostatic copies of this letter and attachment are being forwarded to the Anchorage Field Office. **In accordance with Bureau Bulletin #42, dated July 30, 1947**, Anchorage is requested to interview Captain JACK PECK and his co-pilot, VINCE DALY, **preferably separately**, in order to obtain all details of their reported sighting of a flying object on August 4, 1947. The San Francisco Field Office should be furnished two copies of the results of these interviews for distribution to the Sixth Army Intelligence."

Stamped 62-83894-97 / RECORDED & INDEXED / FBI September 23 1947 / EX-42 / 62-2938.

The Hoover September 28, 1947 follow-up letter (page 181) — Director-to-SAC SF — confirms the routing chain to Anchorage:

> "Enclosed is a copy of a letter dated August 28, 1947, from J. Edgar Hoover to S-A, Anchorage Field Office, with the sighting of a flying object by [redacted] and his co-pilot [redacted] near Alaska. … It is requested that you interview [redacted] and his co-pilot [redacted] in order to obtain all details of the reported sighting of a flying object on August 28, 1947."

The investigative protocol — **separate interviews, two copies of results, distribution to Sixth Army Intelligence** — is the operational instantiation of Bureau Bulletin #42, three weeks after Davidson and Brown died and one week after Springer hand-delivered the additional CIC paperwork to SAC SF on August 27 (page 184 in the pass-23 cluster).

This page closes the pass-23 open thread on the Bethel Alaska NCOIC letter — the substantive case is at page 180, not page 182. Page 182 is the cover routing for a separate (post-Aug 28) Bethel forwarding.

### Case 3 — Lake Mead Armstrong (Section 2 page 189)

MOIC at Williams Field, Chandler, Arizona, dated 15 July 1947. Subject "UNIDENTIFIABLE OBJECTS." Verbatim:

> "On 14 July 1947, 1st Lt Eric B. Armstrong, 0-2059709, 170th AAF Base Unit, Ferry Division, Brooks Field, San Antonio, Texas was interviewed and the following information was obtained: Lt. Armstrong departed Williams Field, Arizona at 1400 CST on 28 June 1947 in a P-51 for Portland, Oregon, by the way of Medford, Oregon. At approximately 1515 CST on a course of 300 degrees, and ground speed of 285, altitude 10,000 feet, approximately thirty miles northwest of Lake Meade [sic — Lake Mead], Nevada Lt. Armstrong sighted five or six white, circular objects at four o'clock, altitude approximately 6,000 feet, course approximately 120 degrees and an estimated speed of 285 MPH."

The pilot's evaluative posture:

> "Lt. Armstrong said the objects were flying very smoothly and in a close formation. The estimated size of the white objects were approximately 36 inches in diameter. **Lt. Armstrong stated that he is sure the white objects were not birds**, since the rate of closure was very fast. **Lt. Armstrong was certain that the white objects were not jets or conventional type aircraft since he has flown both types.**"

Agent's notes:

> "Lt. Armstrong was very sincere in the explanation and was not the exaggerating type. He merely stated what he saw and has drawn no conclusions as to what the white objects were."

Signed: **Lawrence R. King, Jr., Special Agent, CIC, ADC.** Certified a true copy by Major R. D. Stephens, Air Corps. Filed 62-2938-11. Stamped received SF FBI July 30 1947.

This single MOIC closes both pass-23 open threads — it is **the Lawrence R. King Jr. CIC ADC memo of 15 July 1947** referenced in the SAC SF Aug 27 cover (pass-23 page 184), AND the Lt. Armstrong / Lake Mead sighting referenced as Incl 6 in Springer's synopsis (pass-23 page 160).

### Case 4 — Myrtle Creek Hatfield/Ellison (Section 2 page 52)

SAC Portland memo to Bureau, dated 8-23-47. Subject "**FLYING DISCS / SECURITY MATTER - X**" — the SECURITY MATTER - X classification used for early UAP investigation.

> "RAY VIRGIL HATFIELD, Route 1, Box 195, Roseburg, Oregon, operator of the Tri City Airport, Myrtle Creek, Oregon, advised that on 8-6-47 at approximately 6:15 PM **while instructing a student in a take off** at the airport, he noticed an object east of Myrtle Creek which appeared to be from 5,000 to 8,000 feet in the air. The sky was completely clear and visibility was excellent. He stated that the object glistened and appeared to be of aluminium sheeting. Upon noticing it he had immediately taken over the controls of the plane which at that time was at 400 feet, and proceeded East in an attempt to further observe the object. He advised that the object appeared to be climbing and traveling East at a high rate of speed which he estimated **on a computer in his plane as 1,000 miles per hour**. He believed the object to be spherical in shape and recalled noticing a darker object to the right the first time he saw it."

The corroboration on second takeoff:

> "After searching the area for approximately 10 minutes HATFIELD and his student NOBLE ELLISON returned to the airport and made another landing and take off. **In practically the same position at 400 feet altitude, both ELLISON and HATFIELD saw the object in approximately the same position as seen before.** He estimated the sphere to be 30 feet in diameter and stated when they first observed it it appeared to be so near he could fly right to it."

Witness credentials:

> "It is noted that HATFIELD served as a Lieutenant JG in the U. S. Naval Air Corps for about 3 1/2 years, flying constantly on the Atlantic submarine patrol. He is a reputable citizen in Douglas County and is reported to be a qualified pilot."

Ellison's separate corroboration:

> "Mr. NOBLE ELLISON, Myrtle Creek, Oregon, verified the above information as furnished by HATFIELD … ELLISON described the object as a 'silver ball or balloon' which he believed to be 8 miles east of Myrtle Creek, traveling East, and climbing very fast until it disappeared in approximately 45 seconds. ELLISON estimated the sphere as being 50 feet in diameter and stated in his opinion the second object appeared to climb straight up."

Filed 62-83894-65. Stamped EX-34, INDEXED 135, 86 AUG 29 1947. **November 18 1964 destruction stamp ("COPIES DESTROYED 270 NOV 18 1964")** — third confirmed instance of the November 1964 destruction-stamp pattern.

### Case 5 — McLeod Seattle Times / General Hale AAF Policy Statement (Section 2 page 190)

SAC SF memo to Director (Attention: Assistant Director D. M. Ladd), August 27, 1947. The transmission is by phone, not letter — **Colonel Springer telephonically advised SA Michael J. Cassidy of the SF Office at 10:40 AM on August 27, 1947.**

The McLeod letter content (Springer's verbal summary preserved):

> "a personal letter had been received by his organization from HENRY McLEOD, city editor of the Seattle Times, which stated in effect that because there were so many stories going around relative to flying discs and related matters, and that his staff of reporters had been attempting to get reliable information concerning such stories, he was writing to inquire whether any governmental agency was going to make an official statement relative to the situation pertaining to the flying discs; that Mr. McLEOD also asked **whether it would be permissible for his reporters to interview ARNOLD, DAHL, and SMITH** relative to this situation."

The "ARNOLD, DAHL, and SMITH" naming list is striking: Arnold = Kenneth Arnold (Mt. Rainier sighting); Dahl = Harold Dahl (Maury Island); Smith = Capt. E. J. Smith (UAL July 4 1947 sighting). Three witnesses, three separate incidents, all flagged by McLeod's reporting staff as the principal interview targets by August 25 1947.

General Hale's response, on the FBI record:

> "Colonel SPRINGER stated that the above-mentioned letter was answered personally by General HALE on August 25, 1947, and advised Mr. McLEOD that **as far as the Army Air Force was concerned, they had no information which would definitely clear up the situation**; that as far as interviewing persons, **Mr. McLEOD could feel free to interview whomsoever he desired, in that there was no censorship at the present time**; that **in the interest of economy, the Fourth Air Force Headquarters would not run out every lead or report of flying discs**, but that if well-founded and apparently reliable information were received, that such information would be given attention."

Stamped 62-83894-99 / EX-81 / 31 SEP 23 1947 / 60 OCT 1 1947 / "Handed by Stop Desk." Carbon distributed to Mr. Ladd, Mr. Nichols, Mr. Rosen, Mr. Tracy, Mr. Egan, Mr. Guraea, Mr. Harbo, Mr. Mohr, Mr. Pennington, Mr. Quinn Tamm, Mr. Nease, Miss Gandy. **The full Hoover-era distribution list — twelve named senior officials including Tamm and Mohr.**

### Case 6 — Forrest Wenyon Pre-Roswell Pilot Sightings (Section 2 pages 192, 193, 194)

SAC Baltimore memo to Director FBI, dated 9/4/47. Subject "FLYING DISCS / MISCELLANEOUS." Filed 62-0 / 62-83894 (stub). Triggered by the Morning News (Delaware) newspaper clipping of July 8, 1947 with headline:

> **"Flying Mayonnaise Jars Reported by Rehoboth Pilot — Forrest Wenyon Believes Mystery Discs, Jet-Propelled, May Have Caused Big Plane Crash on Memorial Day"**

The clipping content preserved verbatim on page 194. Wenyon's bona fides: Forrest Wenyon, 123 Rehoboth Avenue, Rehoboth Beach, Delaware. **Veteran Delaware pilot, 30 years' flying experience.**

The September 1946 sighting:

> "Mr. Wenyon said that he first saw what loosely resembled a 'flying mayonnaise jar' one afternoon last September while flying at about 1,000 feet. It was late in the afternoon and the pilot was flying north."

> "Suddenly, at an estimated distance of 2,000 feet in front of his plane and traveling from west to east, Mr. Wenyon saw the 'jar.' It was going at tremendous speed but the pilot was able to note several things. The most important of these observations, insofar as an explanation is possible, is that the 'jar' appeared to have some sort of rocket combustion. **Mr. Wenyon said that spurts of silver flame from the tail seemed to provide the propulsion.**"

> "Carrying out the description of the 'flying mayonnaise jar,' Mr. Wenyon said that the tail appeared to be a lid which had been perforated and that from these perforations the flames escaped."

> "In a matter of two, at the most three, seconds the object had disappeared over and past Fort Miles."

The June 2, 1947 sighting:

> "Then on June 2 while flying at 1,400 feet in a Stinson four-passenger ship Mr. Wenyon, a pilot of 30 years' experience, saw another 'jar.'"

The FBI's pre-Bureau-Bulletin-#42 disposition, in primary source:

> "He didn't say much about it to the Rehoboth airport attaches although he did mention it. Mr. Wenyon, with a theory in mind, **called the Federal Bureau of Investigation. They were not interested and told him so**, he said. He then called Eastern Airlines and the Civil Aeronautic Authority both of which gave him a sympathetic hearing, thanked him and said they'd investigate. To date he has received no answer from either."

The Eastern Airlines Memorial Day crash theory:

> "Mr. Wenyon's theory is that one of these 'jars' was responsible for the **crash of the Eastern Airlines plane at Port Deposit, Md., on Memorial Day which killed 53 persons.** He knows the stories of that accident and believes now that the explosion in mid-air, witnessed by a number of witnesses, was caused by one of these jars."

The SAC Baltimore follow-up interview (page 192):

> "I directed Mr. WENYON be interviewed to determine the full facts. Mr. WENYON was interviewed and it was determined that his correct name is HORACE P. WENYON. … He stated that in September of 1946, while flying at an altitude of 1,000 feet, two or three miles south of Rehoboth Beach, Delaware, he noticed a projectile approximately fifteen inches in diameter which crossed his course at right angles and was moving in a west-to-east direction. According to Mr. WENYON, several jets of flame were spurting from the object and it was traveling at a very high rate of speed, **1,000 to 1,200 miles per hour**. He stated that **in October of 1946, he observed a similar projectile while flying over Rehoboth Beach at 1,400 feet**. This was also traveling from west to east."

Wenyon's posture (page 192):

> "Mr. WENYON stated that there is little question in his mind but that what he saw was some sort of rocket being tested. He stated that he wished to call the matter to the attention of the appropriate authority inasmuch as he felt that anyone of these objects falling onto a populated area could result in considerable damage."

The naming-clarification line on page 193:

> "Mr. WENYON stated that what he had seen traveled at such a high rate of speed that it was very difficult to describe it, but **the description 'flying mayonnaise jars' was concocted by the newspaper reporter.**"

Disposition (page 193):

> "This information has not been brought to the attention of any Army sources in view of the fact that only a preliminary inquiry was conducted on the basis of a news article which sets forth essentially the facts obtained from Mr. WENYON. No further action in this matter is contemplated by this office unless advised otherwise by the Bureau."

The newspaper clipping on page 194 also preserves substantial 1947-period broader context — A. B. Cross of Chattanooga TN claiming to have invented the "flying saucer" and submitted it to the War Department in 1943; Charlie T. Hamlet of the Kingsport TN Times News stating he saw discs **two years ago** ("of a bright, aluminum color" and "going at terrific speed") but kept quiet because of the Oak Ridge atomic bomb plant; Bob Johnson of Missoula MT flying service claiming he had captured one of the discs and "found it to be milkweed seeds"; Charles Odom (former B-17 navigator) theorizing that the objects were "radio-controlled crystal balls used by the Nazis in the war"; Lester Barlow of Stamford CT (international explosives inventor) theorizing radio-controlled flying missiles; **Gen. Carl Spaatz, Army Air Forces commandant, saying he knew of no AAF plans to search for the saucers**; the Navy and Atomic Energy Commission saying they had no connection with the mystery.

## Why This Matters

1. **Wenyon establishes the 1946 pre-Roswell pre-Arnold pilot baseline on the FBI record.** A 30-year pilot reported flying-disc-like objects in **September 1946 + October 1946 + June 2, 1947** — the first sighting **ten months before Kenneth Arnold's June 24, 1947 Mt. Rainier sighting and roughly 10 months before any popular awareness of "flying saucers"**. The Bureau preserved the witness's documented experience in primary form. The pre-Roswell record is significantly thicker than the popular framing acknowledges.

2. **The FBI's pre-Bureau-Bulletin-#42 baseline disposition: "they were not interested and told him so."** Wenyon's call to the FBI (date undetermined, between Sept 1946 and July 8 1947 newspaper publication) generated the Bureau's documented reply: not interested. This is the **operational baseline that Bureau Bulletin #42 of July 30, 1947 reversed** — establishing field-office authority to interview UAP witnesses in coordination with the AAF. Wenyon's case is the on-record before-state: post-Bulletin-#42, Anchorage was assigned to interview Captain Peck and Vince Daly (case 2 above) under the new operational protocol.

3. **The Eastern Airlines Memorial Day 1947 plane crash UAP-causation theory enters the Bureau record via Wenyon.** Wenyon's theory that one of his "flying mayonnaise jars" caused the crash of the Eastern Airlines plane at Port Deposit MD on Memorial Day 1947 (53 dead) is in primary source on the file. UAP-causation theorizing of major aviation accidents — a standing recurring claim in the popular UAP corpus — has its earliest in-archive root here, in September 1947 SAC Baltimore Bureau record.

4. **General Hale's August 25, 1947 AAF policy statement establishes the AAF "no censorship" record in writing.** The McLeod-Seattle Times letter (case 5) preserves AAF Commanding General Hale's personal-reply commitments: "no information which would definitely clear up the situation," "no censorship at the present time," "in the interest of economy, the Fourth Air Force Headquarters would not run out every lead or report of flying discs, but that if well-founded and apparently reliable information were received, that such information would be given attention." This is the loaded operational corollary to General Carl Spaatz's contemporaneous "I know of no AAF plans to search for the saucers" quote in the Wenyon clipping (page 194). The popular framing of AAF concealment runs against these written records.

5. **The Bethel Alaska Peck/Daly case closes the pass-23 open thread and instantiates Bureau Bulletin #42 in operational form.** "**In accordance with Bureau Bulletin #42, dated July 30, 1947**, Anchorage is requested to interview Captain JACK PECK and his co-pilot, VINCE DALY, **preferably separately**." The separate-interview protocol — to prevent witness contamination — is on the file as the Bureau's standing procedure as of late August 1947. Two-copy distribution to Sixth Army Intelligence is also recorded.

6. **The Lake Mead Armstrong / King MOIC closes two pass-23 open threads simultaneously.** The single MOIC at Section 2 page 189, certified by Major R. D. Stephens and signed by Special Agent Lawrence R. King Jr. of CIC ADC, is exactly the document referenced as Incl 6 in Springer's synopsis (pass-23 page 160) AND exactly the King memo referenced in the SAC SF Aug 27 cover (pass-23 page 184). One artifact, two pass-23 open threads closed.

7. **The Twin Falls Hedstrom multi-witness police-officer case is the second-largest single-event mass-witness sighting in 1947 in this archive.** With three Twin Falls Police Department officers (Detective Frazier, Rountree, Scott) joining three civilian witnesses across sustained observations of formations of 10, 5-6, 35-50, 12, and ongoing 3/5/7 groups over **20-25 minutes** — this exceeds the Springer cluster's three-object Baker sighting. The SAC Butte's investigator-discipline language is also rare: explicit ruling-out of meteors / comets / geese / ducks / city-light reflection plus the **"none of the persons who claimed to have seen these objects were drinking at the time"** finding is the kind of tradecraft that doesn't appear elsewhere in the 1947 case file.

8. **Myrtle Creek Hatfield/Ellison documents in-flight cross-corroborated sighting from a Naval-Air-Corps-credentialed pilot.** Hatfield's 3.5 years on Atlantic submarine patrol as Lt. JG U.S. Naval Air Corps establishes military-aviation credibility comparable to United Airlines' Capt. E. J. Smith (pass-23 page 174). The two-takeoff observation pattern — sighting on first takeoff, **second sighting on second takeoff at the same approximate position with student Ellison cross-witnessing in real time** — is unusually structured corroboration. Hatfield's aircraft-computer speed estimate of 1,000 MPH is the highest computed speed by a civilian-pilot witness in the 1947 corpus.

9. **Fourth confirmed instance of the "COPIES DESTROYED 270 NOV 18 1964" stamp.** Section 2 pages 52 (Hatfield/Ellison) AND 177 (Hedstrom) both bear the November 18 1964 destruction stamp. Combined with the November 23 1964 stamp on the Frank Scully URGENT teletype (pass 21) and the Kodiak ONI report (pass 22), the Bureau's late-1964 retention housekeeping was **a multi-day general cull** rather than a single-event purge — strengthening the Bureau-wide-retention-housekeeping interpretation against any item-specific retraction reading.

10. **The McLeod-Seattle Times distribution list (page 190) includes 12 named senior FBI officials at carbon-copy level.** Mr. A... [illegible], Clegg, Ladd, Nichols, Rosen, Tracy, Egan, Guraea, Harbo, Mohr, Pennington, Quinn Tamm, Nease, Miss Gandy. The breadth confirms that UAP press-handling at the Hoover-era senior level was operationally normalized — a flying-disc-related press-policy phone call from Springer to SAC SF on August 27, 1947 was distributed to the entire upper-Bureau staff as routine.

## Open Questions

- **Bureau Bulletin #42 of July 30, 1947 itself.** This is now the second pass-archive reference to it (first in the present case 2 / Bethel Alaska, second elsewhere implicit). The Bulletin establishes Bureau field-office authority to interview UAP witnesses in coordination with the AAF. The Bulletin text is presumably in the Bureau's internal-policy archive, not in this PURSUE release. Research target: FBI policy-bulletin holdings.
- **Eastern Airlines Memorial Day 1947 plane crash at Port Deposit MD, 53 dead.** The actual aviation-accident record (NTSB / CAB findings) for the May 30, 1947 crash is reconstructable from outside-archive sources. The substantive question is whether the Bureau recorded any subsequent investigative engagement with Wenyon's UAP-causation theory beyond the SAC Baltimore "preliminary inquiry," or whether it died at the page-193 disposition. Cross-archive FBI 100-series search target.
- **Wenyon's third sighting on June 2, 1947** is mentioned but not described in detail in the case file. The case file describes the September 1946 sighting at length (page 192's narrative) and flags the June 2 1947 as a second-jar sighting at 1,400 ft in a Stinson four-passenger ship, but doesn't reproduce the witness's narrative of what he saw. Open for cross-interview research.
- **Forrest Wenyon's full pre-1946 piloting record** — 30 years' flying experience starting in roughly 1917 puts him in the World War I aviation generation. His airfield(s) of record, his commercial vs. private status, and his 1944-46 wartime flying are all unestablished in this file. Biographical research target.
- **Henry McLeod, city editor of the Seattle Times in August 1947** — biographical research target. McLeod's editorial archive of Seattle Times flying-disc coverage during the post-Arnold August-September 1947 period is a candidate cross-archive resource.
- **General Hale (AAF, August 1947)** — likely Major General Willis H. Hale, but the Bureau record only gives surname. Identification of Gen. Hale's exact title and command in August 1947 is straightforward outside-archive research, but is not done in this case.
- **Twin Falls Idaho Detective Richard A. Frazier, M. E. Rountree, and Richard Scott** — Twin Falls Police Department personnel. Biographical research target. Their sustained joint observation gives this case unusual law-enforcement weight.
- **Captain Jack Peck and co-pilot Vince Daly's actual sighting account** is not preserved in pages 180-181 — only the routing memos. The substantive witness narrative was filed at Anchorage Field Office with copies to Sixth Army Intelligence. Cross-section search target across Sections 3-10 for the Anchorage interview reports.
- **A. B. Cross War Department 1943 flying-saucer submission** (Wenyon clipping page 194) — claim that Cross submitted a "flying saucer" idea to the War Department in 1943 and it was rejected as not practical. War Department patent / submission archive research target.
- **Charlie T. Hamlet Kingsport TN Times News two-year-prior 1945 sighting** (Wenyon clipping page 194) — Hamlet kept quiet about a 1945 disc sighting because of the Oak Ridge atomic bomb plant. Cross-references the Oak Ridge / Presley case (`2026-05-08-fbi-62hq83894-oak-ridge-gasser-atomic-propulsion-1947-1949`). Worth follow-up.
- **Bob Johnson Missoula Montana "milkweed seed" recovery** (Wenyon clipping page 194) — claim of physical recovery of one of the discs identified as milkweed seeds. Pre-Maury-Island physical-recovery claim. Newspaper / Missoula civil aviation archive research target.
- **The November 18 1964 destruction stamp on three different Section 2 pages (52, 177)** is consistent with the November 23 1964 stamps from passes 21-22. Five-day window (Nov 18 - Nov 23 1964) — if a Bureau-wide retention purge ran across this window, it would be documented in Bureau records-management policy archives.

## Quotes Worth Keeping

> "None of the persons who claimed to have seen these objects were drinking at the time."
> — SAC Butte memo to Director, September 2, 1947, Section 2 page 179. The investigator-discipline footer to the Hedstrom Twin Falls multi-witness sighting. The kind of explicit-ruling-out tradecraft language that is rare in the 1947 case-file corpus.

> "Detective FRAZIER and Mr. HEDSTROM both stated that they could not be meteors or comets, that they flew on a level line and did not appear to drop. They flew at a terrific rate of speed and were visible for only a few minutes at a time. Both persons interviewed said that the objects could not have been geese or ducks and that the lights were not a reflection of the city lights on some object or objects."
> — SAC Butte memo, ibid., Section 2 page 179. The Twin Falls Police detective's explicit non-anomalous-explanation rejection list, on the Bureau record.

> "In accordance with Bureau Bulletin #42, dated July 30, 1947, Anchorage is requested to interview Captain JACK PECK and his co-pilot, VINCE DALY, preferably separately, in order to obtain all details of their reported sighting of a flying object on August 4, 1947."
> — SAC San Francisco to Director FBI, September 4, 1947, Section 2 page 180. The earliest in-archive primary-source instantiation of Bureau Bulletin #42's separate-witness-interview protocol.

> "Lt. Armstrong stated that he is sure the white objects were not birds, since the rate of closure was very fast. Lt. Armstrong was certain that the white objects were not jets or conventional type aircraft since he has flown both types."
> — Lawrence R. King, Jr., Special Agent, CIC, ADC, MOIC at Williams Field, 15 July 1947, Section 2 page 189. The AAF P-51 pilot's evaluative posture on his June 28 1947 Lake Mead sighting.

> "Lt. Armstrong was very sincere in the explanation and was not the exaggerating type. He merely stated what he saw and has drawn no conclusions as to what the white objects were."
> — Lawrence R. King, Jr., ibid. The CIC ADC agent's evaluative posture on Armstrong, parallel to Brown's evaluative posture on Arnold.

> "[HATFIELD] estimated [the object's speed] on a computer in his plane as 1,000 miles per hour."
> — SAC Portland memo, August 23, 1947, Section 2 page 52. The highest computed speed by a civilian-pilot witness in the 1947 case-file corpus, computed in real-time on aircraft instrumentation.

> "as far as the Army Air Force was concerned, they had no information which would definitely clear up the situation; that as far as interviewing persons, Mr. McLEOD could feel free to interview whomsoever he desired, in that there was no censorship at the present time; that in the interest of economy, the Fourth Air Force Headquarters would not run out every lead or report of flying discs, but that if well-founded and apparently reliable information were received, that such information would be given attention."
> — General Hale (AAF) personal reply to Henry McLeod, Seattle Times city editor, August 25, 1947, as transcribed by Col. Springer to SF FBI on August 27, 1947, Section 2 page 190. The earliest in-archive primary-source AAF policy statement on flying-disc reporting and press access.

> "Mr. WENYON, with a theory in mind, called the Federal Bureau of Investigation. They were not interested and told him so, he said."
> — Morning News (Delaware) clipping, July 8, 1947, preserved at Section 2 page 194 in the SAC Baltimore September 4, 1947 case file. The pre-Bureau-Bulletin-#42 baseline FBI disposition on UAP witness-call intake, in primary source.

> "Mr. Wenyon's theory is that one of these 'jars' was responsible for the crash of the Eastern Airlines plane at Port Deposit, Md., on Memorial Day which killed 53 persons. He knows the stories of that accident and believes now that the explosion in mid-air, witnessed by a number of witnesses, was caused by one of these jars."
> — Morning News clipping, ibid., Section 2 page 194. The earliest in-archive UAP-causation theorizing of a major aviation accident.

> "the description 'flying mayonnaise jars' was concocted by the newspaper reporter."
> — Forrest Wenyon, interviewed by SAC Baltimore, September 4, 1947, Section 2 page 193. The witness explicitly disowning the most colorful headline-language attached to his case — a procedural data point on press-distortion of UAP testimony.

> "[Hamlet] saw the discs two years ago … kept quiet about them because of the Oak Ridge atomic bomb plant, then a war secret."
> — Charlie T. Hamlet, Kingsport TN Times News composing-room superintendent, quoted in the Morning News clipping, Section 2 page 194. **A 1945 wartime UAP sighting near Oak Ridge, by a witness who explicitly self-suppressed for atomic-security reasons.** Establishes the pre-Roswell UAP-near-vital-installations pattern that traces forward into the 1947-1949 Oak Ridge / Gasser / Presley case (`2026-05-08-fbi-62hq83894-oak-ridge-gasser-atomic-propulsion-1947-1949`).
