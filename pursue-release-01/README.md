## TL;DR

The May 8, 2026 PURSUE Release 01 contains 162 files at war.gov/UFO/. Most coverage led with a viral football-shaped infrared blob and a cop's "Eye of Sauron" simile. Both are real cases. Neither is the actual story.

The actual story has four threads:

1. **[Apollo 17 (NASA-UAP-VM6).](cases/apollo-17-vm6.md)** A photo from the lunar surface in December 1972 shows three dots in a triangular formation. The government's new official language is *"potentially the result of a physical object."* Original film has been retrieved. Re-analysis pending. This is the only file in the release where AARO's standard deflationary phrasing is replaced with something stronger.

2. **[September 2023 Bronze Ellipsoid (FBI).](cases/bronze-ellipsoid.md)** An ellipsoid bronze metallic object, 130 to 195 feet long, *"materialized out of a bright light in the sky"* at a US test site, *"disappearing instantaneously."* Multi-witness, three FBI 302 interviews, FBI Lab composite-sketch overlay on actual site photo. Most evidentially structured single case in the release. Almost no breakout coverage.

3. **[The USPER Statement.](cases/usper-statement.md)** A senior US intelligence official, in an FBI 302 interview, describes a *"super-hot"* orb that outran a pursuit helicopter for 20 miles at a US military facility in late 2025, followed by 30 minutes of orbs *"flaring up and down"* across the area. Highest-rank single witness in the release.

4. **[The Western US "Orbs Launching Orbs" 2023 case.](cases/orbs-launching-orbs.md)** Seven federal law enforcement officers, three two-officer teams, watched orange orbs emit smaller red orbs at least five times across two days. AARO's own self-assessment, in the official slides PDF, is that this case is *"among the most compelling within AARO's current holdings."* That is unusually direct language for AARO.

The release also contains a [Mexican alien-corpses State Department cable](cases/mexico-2003-alien-corpses.md) (Mexico Congress, 2003), a [1994 Kazakhstan 747 encounter cable](cases/kazakhstan-1994-747.md), the COMETA report, the 1944 "foo fighters" SHAEF file, a Kennedy-era memo on alien diplomatic policy, and AARO's own admissions that some cases in the release have already been resolved as Russian aircraft (PR-21) or platform parallax (PR-45).

Read carefully, this is not nothing. Read carefully, it is also not aliens. It is the most evidentially structured public-domain UAP material in decades, with several genuinely strange cases buried under viral noise.

## The four threads that matter

### 1: NASA-UAP-VM6, Apollo 17 Triangular Formation Photo

→ [Full case writeup](cases/apollo-17-vm6.md)

The strongest non-deflationary commitment in the entire release. An Apollo 17 lunar-surface photo (December 1972) showing three dots in a triangular formation in the lower right quadrant of the sky.

AARO's verbatim language:

> "New preliminary US government analysis suggests the image feature is potentially the result of a physical object in the scene. Additionally, as part of this investigation, the government has obtained the original film from the Apollo 17 mission and the results of the full NASA and DOW analysis will be released when completed."

Two things to notice:

- *"Potentially the result of a physical object"* is a deliberate softening of the boilerplate language used everywhere else in the release.
- *"Obtained the original film"* implies access to material not previously rescanned under modern techniques.

The companion **Apollo 17 voice transcript (NASA-UAP-D2)** has Cdr Eugene Cernan describing *"an intense light flashing between his eyes"* comparable to *"a train headlight,"* characterized as *"imposing."* Over three hours he reported *"several flashing, rotating phenomena"* he assessed as physical objects rather than purely optical. Schmitt observed similar phenomena and exclaimed about *"a flash on the lunar surface north of Grimaldi (crater)."*

Apollo 12 (NASA-UAP-D1) has LMP Alan Bean observing *"particles and flashes of light 'sailing off in space'"* via the Alignment Optical Telescope, characterized as *"escaping the Moon."*

This is the case to track. If the Apollo 17 re-analysis lands on "is" rather than "potentially," it changes the disclosure conversation in a way no infrared sensor clip can.

### 2: The September 2023 Bronze Ellipsoid (FBI)

→ [Full case writeup](cases/bronze-ellipsoid.md)

The most evidentially layered case in the release. Multi-witness FBI investigation of an apparent ellipsoid bronze metallic object that *"materialized out of a bright light in the sky"* at a US test site in September 2023, *"disappearing instantaneously."*

Object size, per the FBI Lab composite-sketch description: **130 to 195 feet in length.** That is the rough length of a Boeing 737 to a Boeing 767.

Three FBI 302 interview reports corroborate:

- One witness: *"metallic bronze in color"*
- Another witness: *"metallic/gray in color"*
- A third witness: *"bright light over the horizon"*

The FBI Lab produced an actual graphic overlay on a real photograph of the site. That kind of evidentiary artifact is rare across the entire release.

Almost no major outlet is leading with this case. The football-shaped infrared blob is dominating coverage instead, despite being a 9-second monocular sensor clip without scale, range, or witness corroboration.

### 3: The USPER Statement

→ [Full case writeup](cases/usper-statement.md)

Highest-rank single witness in the release. An FBI 302 interview with a senior US intelligence official describing a first-hand UAP encounter at a US military facility in late 2025.

The narrative:

- Federal and state personnel searched an area where orbs had been previously seen.
- Helicopter deployed.
- Found a *"super-hot"* orb hovering over the ground.
- Orb traveled 20 miles *"at a speed too fast for the helicopter in pursuit."*
- A *"swarm"* of lights then moved in all directions.
- Four or five additional orbs flared up, then down.
- This pattern continued for 30 minutes across the area.

A senior intelligence official, on the record, describes a sustained 30-minute multi-orb event at a US military facility, with helicopter pursuit failure and thermal observation. If accurate as filed, this is the kind of incident where the government has all the supporting sensor data and is choosing what to release. The 302 interview is what they released. The helicopter's onboard FLIR, radar, and thermal imagery is, presumably, what they didn't.

### 4: The Western US "Orbs Launching Orbs" Event (2023)

→ [Full case writeup](cases/orbs-launching-orbs.md)

Seven federal law enforcement officers, three two-officer teams, multiple western US states, two days, 2023. Each team independently described orange "orbs" launching smaller red "orbs" in groups of two to four, at least five times.

AARO's own self-assessment in the slides PDF:

> "the reporters' credibility, and the potentially anomalous nature of the events themselves, combine to make this report among the most compelling within AARO's current holdings."

That is an unusually direct AARO endorsement. Compare to the AARO standard line on every video clip: "should not be interpreted as a conclusive indication."

The viral "Eye of Sauron" simile that has dominated coverage is one of these officers' attempt to describe what he saw using a cultural reference. The official AARO shape characterization is "orange orb" emitting smaller "red orbs," not Eye of Sauron.

## Other substantive files most coverage has missed

### State Dept Cable 5, Mexico, September 2003: Alien Corpses Hearing

→ [Full case writeup](cases/mexico-2003-alien-corpses.md)

Verbatim AARO summary:

> "On September 12, 20023 [sic, 2003] the Mexican Congress heard testimony on UAP from experts related to the debate about an Aerial Space Protection Law … if approved [it] would make Mexico the first country to formally acknowledge the presence of alien life on earth. They presented two alleged alien corpses and videos of Mexican pilot's encounters … Disagreement about the efficacy and validity of the purported alien corpses."

A US Embassy cable, on file at the Department of State, documenting the Mexican Congress being shown alleged alien corpses in 2003. Buried in a 162-file release.

### State Dept Cable 2, Kazakhstan, January 1994, 747 Encounter

→ [Full case writeup](cases/kazakhstan-1994-747.md)

Tajik commercial pilot plus three American citizens, flying a 747 at 41,000 feet. Object made *"90 degree turns, doing corkscrews and maneuvering in circles a great rates of speed."* Photos taken. Visual estimation of contrails at 100,000 feet, too high for any known 1994 conventional aircraft.

### COMETA Report (inclusion itself is the story)

The 1999 French Institute of Higher Studies for National Defence COMETA report, which argued openly that UAP were likely extraterrestrial and that France should prepare. Filed in PURSUE under document 255_413270, with redaction flag = TRUE. The fact that the US government chose to include this hawkish foreign report in its first transparency tranche is itself a signal worth reading.

### The 1944 "Foo Fighters" SHAEF File

Document 331_120752, dated 3/18/1945, Germany. SHAEF messages and memos on *"night phenomena (foofighters), flak rockets, unidentified cylindrical objects, and blinking lights,"* with multiple references to the 415th Night Fighter Squadron. The original "foo fighters" archive, declassified.

### The Kennedy-Era Alien Diplomatic Policy Memo

Document 59_214434 SP 16, dated 7/18/1963. A memo from the Executive Office of the President, National Aeronautics and Space Council, on *"thoughts on the space alien race question."* Includes *"plans if alien intelligence is discovered, expanding scientific knowledge, the possibility of life on Mars, and diplomatic policy."* Kennedy administration thought through alien-contact diplomatic policy on paper.

### Wladyslaw Krasuski 1944 Germany

FBI report from November 7, 1957, documenting an interview with a witness who described a *"large, circular, vertically-rising vehicle in 1944 Germany near a German military compound."* Filed officially with the FBI 13 years after the alleged sighting.

## What AARO is quietly admitting

The release contains AARO's own commentary on selected cases. These are revealing because they show the office openly identifying mundane causes inside the "unresolved" tranche:

- **DOW-UAP-PR21 (Iraq 2022).** Mission report described UAP as "probable SU-27/35." AARO comment: "SU-27 and SU-35 are designations for military aircraft operated by the Armed Forces of the Russian Federation." (Russian fighter, not anomaly.)
- **DOW-UAP-PR45 (Southern US 2020).** AARO comment: "The area of contrast's apparent increase in size is likely to be at least partially attributable to the U.S. platform closing the distance between itself and the source of the detection." (Parallax artifact.)
- **DOW-UAP-PR38 (Middle East 2013).** Modal Image text reads "Resolved as an Aircraft, Middle East 2013", included in the unresolved tranche despite already being resolved.

The implication: AARO is willing to identify mundane causes when it can. The cases left unresolved are unresolved because the data is insufficient, not because AARO is hiding alien explanations behind boilerplate. This is the strongest argument against the "they're sitting on the truth" framing.

It also means the cases that survive AARO's filter. Apollo 17 VM6, the Bronze Ellipsoid, USPER, Western US orbs, survive for a reason.

## What this release does not contain

- No physical specimen.
- No publicly committed identification of any object as non-natural.
- No release of sensor metadata that would let independent analysts replicate AARO's work on the unresolved cases.
- No Anna Paulina Luna 46-video whistleblower batch (expected in a later tranche).
- No new commitment beyond *"potentially the result of a physical object"* on Apollo 17.

By Tyson's standard ("preferably alive"), the release is anticlimactic. By any practical evidentiary-research standard, it is the largest official UAP file dump in US history and contains several cases that warrant follow-up.

Both can be true.
