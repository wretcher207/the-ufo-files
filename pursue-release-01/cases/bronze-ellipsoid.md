## Summary

Most evidentially structured single case in PURSUE Release 01. Multi-witness FBI investigation of an apparent ellipsoid bronze metallic object that "materialized out of a bright light in the sky" at a US test site in September 2023. Object size: 130-195 feet in length. Object disappeared instantaneously. FBI Lab produced a composite sketch overlay on a real site photograph. Three 302-form interview reports (two US citizens, one named USPER) corroborate.

The release contains:
- **FBI Composite Sketch** (2024-04-30): real site photo with FBI Lab graphic overlay rendering the object based on corroborating eyewitness accounts.
- **Serial 3** (FBI 302): US citizen describes object "metallic bronze in color."
- **Serial 4** (FBI 302): US citizen describes object "metallic/gray in color."
- **Serial 5** (FBI 302): US citizen describes "bright light over the horizon."

## Verbatim AARO Description (Composite Sketch)

> "Actual site photo with FBI Lab rendered graphic overlay depicting corroborating eyewitness reports from September 2023 of an apparent ellipsoid bronze metallic object materializing out of a bright light in the sky, 130-195 feet in length, and disappearing instantaneously."

## Why This Case Is Underrated

1. Most PURSUE items are single-witness or single-sensor. This case has at least three independent eyewitness statements, all formalized as FBI 302s.
2. The FBI Lab produced an actual graphic overlay on the real photograph. That is a non-trivial evidentiary artifact most cases lack.
3. Specific object dimensions: 130-195 feet long. That's the rough length of a Boeing 737 (110 ft) to a Boeing 757 (155 ft) to a Boeing 767 (180 ft). Eyewitness convergence on a precise size band is rare.
4. "Materializing out of a bright light … disappearing instantaneously" rules out conventional aircraft kinematics. That's the kind of phenomenon the AARO process is designed to investigate.
5. Despite all of the above, the case has near-zero breakout coverage. Most stories are reading the football and Eye-of-Sauron items.

## Cross-Reference

The "USPER Statement" in this same release describes a senior US intelligence official's first-hand account at a US military facility, with super-hot orbs and helicopter pursuit. Its release alongside the Bronze Ellipsoid (also a US-test-site case, also late 2023 / late 2025) suggests a linked western US testing-grounds context worth tracking.

The FBI Photo B-series (24 monochrome stills, late 2025, "Western United States") may be the sensor-platform record of related encounters.
