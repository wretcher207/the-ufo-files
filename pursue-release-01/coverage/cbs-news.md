## Summary

CBS overview of the May 8, 2026 PURSUE release. Most substantive coverage of the actual file contents: 162 files, specific incidents, and the Apollo 17 triangular formation analysis (the closest thing to a smoking gun in the release). Pentagon's official line that no investigation has confirmed extraterrestrial life is included alongside the most striking case descriptions.

## Key Points

### Volume and Distribution
- 162 files total: 120 PDFs, 28 videos, 14 image files.
- Hosted at war.gov/UFO/, rolling release schedule.
- Multi-agency: DoD, FBI, NASA, ODNI, Department of Energy, AARO.

### The Apollo 17 Triangular Formation
- Photo from lunar surface (Dec 1972) shows three dots in a triangular formation visible when magnified.
- Government's "new preliminary analysis" suggests the feature is "potentially the result of a physical object."
- Original Apollo 17 film has been obtained for full re-analysis.
- This is the case most likely to break out of the news cycle.

### Most Striking Recent Cases
- **Greece, 2023**: Object making 90-degree turns at ~80 mph.
- **Indo-Pacific, 2024**: Football-shaped object near Japan.
- **Syria, October 2024**: Two semi-transparent orange areas, two seconds each.
- **Mediterranean**: Pilot reports triangular metallic UAP at 25,000 ft.
- **Western US, 2023**: Federal officers describe an orb "similar to the Eye of Sauron from Lord of the Rings, except without the pupil, or maybe an orange Storm Electrify bowling ball."

### Historical Cases
- **Roswell, 1947**: FBI memo describing a "hexagonal in shape" disc suspended from a balloon.
- **Tajikistan, 1994**: Cable describing a commercial pilot crew witnessing corkscrews and 90-degree turns.

### Official Position
- Hegseth: "These files, hidden behind classifications, have long fueled justified speculation."
- Pentagon: "no evidence that any government investigation into UAPs has confirmed the existence of extraterrestrial life."
- Trump personally said he is uncertain whether aliens exist.
