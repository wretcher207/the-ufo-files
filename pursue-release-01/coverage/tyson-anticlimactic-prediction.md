## Summary

Tyson's pre-release NYT op-ed, summarized by The Hill on May 6, 2026. Two days before the PURSUE drop, Tyson called the outcome correctly: "I expect the alien files will be anticlimactic." The argument is the cleanest evidentiary skepticism in mainstream coverage.

## Key Points

### The Core Claim
"I expect the alien files will be anticlimactic."

### The Reasoning
After "a parade of alien insiders and whistle-blowers testified under oath to Congress in 2023, 2024 and 2025, what's left to learn?"

Public expectations have been pushed past what document disclosure can satisfy. Documents and infrared clips will not move opinion past where congressional testimony already left it.

### What Would Actually Constitute Proof
Tyson said he would "be delighted if the files were accompanied by an actual alien. Preferably alive."

The argument: a real specimen would settle the question definitively, the way no one debates the existence of elephants. Anything short of that leaves the question where it has been for decades.

### Why This Frame Is Useful
- Calibrated to actual evidence, not partisan position.
- Doesn't require the speaker to claim aliens definitely don't exist.
- Shifts the question from "is the government hiding aliens?" to "what would non-document evidence look like?"

## Relevance to David's Work

Best single rhetorical frame for posts that don't want to either dunk on the disclosure or pretend the files settle anything. Tyson's "preferably alive" line is the kind of dry, calibrated humor that fits David's voice.
