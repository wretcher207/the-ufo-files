## Structural Read

AARO's deflationary language is consistent throughout. Every video description ends with: "Readers should not interpret any part of this description as reflecting an analytical judgment, investigative conclusion, or factual determination regarding the described event's validity, nature, or significance." Every mission report ends with: "All descriptive and estimative language contained in this report reflects the reporter's subjective interpretation at the time of the event. Such characterizations should not be interpreted as a conclusive indication of the presence or absence of any intrinsic object features or performance characteristics."

This is the AARO methodological signature. It works two ways: (1) it prevents the office from being read as endorsing extraordinary claims, (2) it lets the office publish striking material without committing to it.

The Apollo 17 VM6 case is the only one where AARO's language softens to "potentially the result of a physical object" — the strongest commitment in the entire release.
