OFFICE MEMORANDUM - UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT

D. C. , [illegible]

FLYING DISCS

Special Agent W. G. Reynolds of the Intelligence Division discussed the possibility that the flying discs might be a product of the Air Force. It should be noted that Intelligence Division personnel agreed with this possibility. It was pointed out to the Intelligence Division personnel that this was the truth, and that they had reported the facts as they appeared in the compiled data.

Colonel [illegible] advised that Reynolds would institute a further check and he was now satisfied that the flying discs, or the Division of Intelligence, could not be considered a development section of the Air Force.

Here is attached a letter addressed to the Director, dated September 3, 1947, from George F. [illegible], which states that the Air Force has no complete survey of the flying discs with which to [illegible] the facts.

INFORMATION:

It is recommended that this memorandum be forwarded to the Intelligence Section for their information.
