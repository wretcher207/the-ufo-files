# 65_HS1-834228961_62-HQ-83894_Section_2 - Page 130

Office Memorandum • UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT

DATE: August 1, 1947
FROM: D.M. Ladd
TO: Director
SUBJECT: FLYING DISCS

Special Agent [redacted] of the liaison section advised that the Air Force intelligence personnel concerned with the above mentioned flying discs were highly classified and that he could not furnish the Federal Bureau of Investigation with any information. He stated it was his opinion that it was a matter for Air Force intelligence.

Regarding this situation based on information received from [redacted] of the Air Force intelligence, it is noted that the Air Force has been unable to identify these flying discs. They have reported sightings of these objects and have pointed out that there have been individuals who have observed these objects and have reported them to the Air Force. It is the conclusion of this individual that there is no evidence that these objects are of a nature that would be of concern to the Federal Government.

It is the conclusion of this individual that if the United States Government is not concerned with these sightings, it does not seem reasonable that the Federal Bureau of Investigation should continue with inquiries in this regard. It is indicated that the report and findings of the Air Force are being forwarded to the Federal Bureau of Investigation.
