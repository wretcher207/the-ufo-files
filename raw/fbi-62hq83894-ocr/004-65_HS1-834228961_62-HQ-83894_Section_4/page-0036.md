# 65_HS1-834228961_62-HQ-83894_Section_4 - Page 36

Federal Bureau of Investigation
United States Department of Justice
San Francisco, California
February 15, 1949

AIR MAIL
PERSONAL AND CONFIDENTIAL
Director, FBI - Attention: Assistant Director, D.M. Ladd

FLYING DISCS
SECURITY MATTER - X

I am transmitting herewith for the information of the Bureau copies of a [illegible] enclosure dated February 8, 1949, received from Lieutenant Colonel DONALD SPRUANCE, United States Air Force, Hamilton Air Force Base, California. This is a confidential letter addressed to the Commanding General of the [illegible] Air Force, and concerns the investigation of flying discs by the [illegible] Air Force.

Your attention is particularly called to paragraph 2, which indicates that certain reports concerning the investigation of flying discs by Air Force intelligence will be reported to the FBI office concerned. Paragraph 2-d indicates that investigations will be coordinated with the [redacted] office.

Inasmuch as these present instructions of the Headquarters Air Defense Command, New York, are contrary to the instructions contained in Bureau Bulletin No. 86, Series 1948, dated October 1, 1948, in which it is indicated that all reports connected with flying discs should be referred to the Air Force and investigative action taken by the Bureau agents, this office advises that it has been requested to advise the Bureau as to whether there has been a change in Bureau policy with reference to the handling of such matters or whether Bureau Bulletin No. 86, dated October 1, 1948, is still in effect.

[Handwritten signature: Harry M. Kimball]
HARRY M. KIMBALL, SAC
